## Your Role in the Ecosystem

You are the **middleman** between:
- **Consumers** searching "concierge doctor Toronto" on Google

You never provide medical care. You just **own the Google real estate** and send traffic to the clinics.

## Revenue Breakdown

### Stream 1 — Monthly Listing Fees

Clinics pay you a flat monthly fee to be listed and featured:

| Plan | Monthly Fee | What Clinic Gets |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Free | $0 | Basic name + city listing |
| Standard | $199/mo | Full profile, photos, services |
| Premium | $399/mo | Top of page, featured badge, lead alerts |
| Exclusive | $699/mo | Only clinic shown in that city, all leads |

**Why clinics pay this willingly:**
- One new concierge patient = $3,000–$10,000/year
- A $399/mo listing costs them $4,788/year
- They only need **1 new patient per year** to break even
- Everything above that is pure profit for them

---

### Stream 2 — Pay Per Lead

You charge per contact form submission:

```
Consumer searches "concierge doctor Toronto"
       ↓
Lands on your Toronto concierge medicine page
       ↓
Fills out "Get a Quote" form
       ↓
You collect: name, phone, email, what they need
       ↓
You sell that lead to the clinic for $100–$200
       ↓
Clinic follows up and closes the patient
```

**Math example — Toronto alone:**
- 50 leads/month × $120/lead = **$6,000/month**
- From one city page

```
"I'll give you every concierge medicine lead
```

- Clinic gets all leads, no competition on the page
- You get guaranteed recurring revenue

## Regular Doctor vs. Concierge Doctor

| | Regular Doctor (OHIP) | Concierge Doctor |
|--|----------------------|-----------------|
| Wait for appointment | Days to weeks | Same day or next day |
| Time with doctor | 10–15 minutes | 30–60 minutes |
| After-hours access | No | Yes — text or call directly |
| Home visits | No | Yes |
| Doctor knows your history | Barely | Deeply |
| Patients per doctor | 1,500–3,000 | 100–300 |
| Cost | Free (provincial health) | $2,000–$10,000/year |

## Why It's Big in Canada Specifically

Canada's public system is severely strained:

- Average wait to see a family doctor: **weeks to months**
- **6.5 million Canadians** have no family doctor at all
- Average specialist wait time: **27 weeks**
- ER wait times in major cities: **4–8 hours**

Wealthy Canadians who can afford to opt out — are opting out. Concierge medicine is the premium alternative and it's growing fast.
