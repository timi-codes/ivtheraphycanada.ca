Toronto has one of Canada's most competitive IV therapy markets, with dozens of clinics offering everything from basic hydration to NAD+ protocols. Here's how to find the best option for your needs.

## What to Look for in a Toronto IV Therapy Clinic

**Medical oversight** — The best clinics employ MDs or NPs who can assess your suitability for IV therapy, monitor your infusion, and respond to adverse reactions. Avoid clinics that let unqualified staff administer IVs.

**Transparent pricing** — Reputable clinics publish their prices. Be wary of clinics that push expensive "packages" before discussing your goals.

**Clean, clinical environment** — IV therapy should be administered in a clean, properly equipped space with sterile supplies.

**Registered providers** — Check that the administering nurse or practitioner is registered with the College of Nurses of Ontario (CNO) or the applicable regulatory body.

## Popular IV Therapy Services in Toronto

### Hydration & Vitamin IV ($150–$300)
The most common entry point. Saline or Lactated Ringer's base with added B vitamins, vitamin C, and electrolytes. Great for general wellness, jet lag, or post-event recovery.

### Myers' Cocktail ($175–$350)
The classic IV wellness formula: magnesium, calcium, B vitamins, and vitamin C. Named after physician John Myers, it's used for fatigue, migraines, and immune support.

### NAD+ Therapy ($400–$800/session)
Increasingly popular in Toronto's wellness scene. NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme linked to cellular energy and aging. Protocols for addiction recovery can span multiple sessions.

### Mobile IV in Toronto ($250–$500)
Several Toronto providers offer mobile IV therapy — they come to your home, office, or hotel. Premium for the convenience, but popular for hangover recovery and busy executives.

## Toronto Neighbourhoods With IV Clinics

- **King West / Entertainment District** — Most concentrated area for IV clinics
- **Yorkville** — Premium wellness clinics catering to a higher-end market
- **North York / Midtown** — Multiple medical spas offering IV alongside aesthetics
- **Scarborough / East Toronto** — More affordable options farther from downtown

## Questions to Ask Before Booking

1. Who administers the IV? What are their credentials?
2. What exactly is in the bag?
3. Do you do a health intake/screening before the infusion?
4. What's your protocol if I have an adverse reaction?
5. Are your supplies sterile and single-use?
