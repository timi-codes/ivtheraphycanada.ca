## What Affects IV Therapy Pricing?

**1. Service type** — Basic hydration is always cheaper than specialty infusions like NAD+. Chelation therapy protocols can require 20–40 sessions.

**2. Provider credentials** — Clinics staffed by MDs and NPs typically charge more than those run by RNs, and for good reason — advanced providers can handle complications.

**3. Location** — Toronto and Vancouver clinics are typically 20–40% more expensive than clinics in smaller cities.

**4. Mobile vs. clinic** — Mobile IV therapy (delivered to your home or office) adds a convenience fee of $50–$150.

**5. Package deals** — Most clinics offer discounted multi-session packages, especially for NAD+ protocols.

## Does Insurance Cover IV Therapy in Canada?

Generally, no. Provincial health insurance (OHIP, MSP, AHCIP, etc.) does not cover IV therapy for wellness purposes.

Some extended health benefit plans may cover IV therapy if:
- It's prescribed by a physician for a medical condition
- Administered by a licensed healthcare provider
- You have a naturopathic or alternative medicine benefit

Always check with your insurer before booking.

## Is IV Therapy Worth It?

The evidence for IV therapy in healthy individuals is mixed. However, clinical applications — especially NAD+ for addiction recovery, chelation for heavy metal toxicity, and high-dose vitamin C for immune support — have more robust backing.

If you're considering IV therapy:
1. Consult your physician first
2. Verify the clinic's credentials
3. Ask what's in the IV bag
4. Get a quote before committing
